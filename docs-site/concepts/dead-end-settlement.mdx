Not every dispute has a valid answer. When all values are disqualified, the final plurality
ties, or no proposer ever submitted a fact, the oracle reaches **`InvalidDeadend`** (Phase 8)
â€” a terminal non-outcome. This page covers why a dead-end happens, exactly what settles, and
how governance can (optionally) stamp a final option afterward. It is grounded in the
dead-end settlement design (`docs/plans/2026-06-30-kassandra-deadend-settlement.md`).

On a dead-end, `Oracle.resolved_option` is stamped with the `CLAIM_OPTION_NONE` sentinel
(`0xFF`), and `reward_pool` is `0`.

## What happens to the money

A dead-end is a **non-outcome: no rewards, no distribution.** The settlement rule (a locked
user decision in the design) is:

- **Non-slashed principal is returned** to each staker via the normal `claim_*` pulls.
- **Slashed amounts (`bond_pool`) are BURNED.** A misbehavior slash has no recipient when
  there is no winner, so it is burned like the creator fee â€” including the **no-facts case,
  where every disputing proposer's bond is burned** (a deterrent against
  propose-conflict-then-abandon).
- **The `reward_emission` is burned** too (there is nothing to reward).
- **The creator fee stays burned**, as always.

Concretely, the burns happen at the finalize sites (`finalize_oracle` and
`finalize_no_facts`): the `InvalidDeadend` branches burn `reward_emission + bond_pool` from
the `stake_vault`, oracle-PDA-signed. After the burns, the vault holds exactly the
returnable principal, and the per-staker claims drain it to dust:

`Î£ returned principal + dust + (bond_pool burned) + (emission burned) == Î£ stakes + emission`.

<Info>
  Because `reward_pool == 0` on a dead-end, every reward term in the claim formulas is
  automatically zero â€” the existing `claim_*` path already pays **non-slashed principal
  only** on `InvalidDeadend`. Disposition matters, though: a rejected-fact submitter and a
  slashed approve-voter get their slashed portion **withheld** (their KASS was in the
  now-burned `bond_pool`), while agreed/duplicate stakers get their full stake back. A
  disqualified proposer gets `0` (their bond was burned); a survivor gets
  `bond âˆ’ slashed_amount`.
</Info>

## Governance-only resolution

A dead-ended oracle can be given a final answer, but **only by governance**. The DAO calls
`resolve_deadend` (`Ix::ResolveDeadend = 15`), which requires `assert_dao_authority`, the
oracle to be in `InvalidDeadend`, and `option < options_count` (`resolve_deadend.rs`). It
advances `InvalidDeadend â†’ Resolved` and records the governance-chosen `resolved_option`
for downstream consumers.

<Warning>
  `resolve_deadend` moves **no tokens.** The dead-end funds were already burned at finalize,
  and a governance-resolved oracle pays **identically** to a plain `InvalidDeadend` (both
  fully drain to non-slashed principal) precisely because `reward_pool == 0` zeroes every
  reward term on both phases. The governance option is recorded for consumers but does
  **not** drive any reward or slash.
</Warning>

Governance is ideally itself run via MetaDAO futarchy (design Â§8). The `dao_authority` is a
Squads v4 multisig vault PDA, established once through the adminâ†’DAO handoff in
`set_governance` (`Ix=13`).

## Cleanup after a dead-end

Like any terminal oracle, a dead-ended oracle is cleaned up permissionlessly: `claim_*`
drains the returnable principal to each staker (closing their accounts), `close_ai_claim`
and `close_market` reclaim metadata/market rent, and after a 30-day grace
(`SWEEP_GRACE = 2_592_000`) `sweep_oracle` transfers any residual vault KASS to the DAO
treasury and closes the oracle and its vault.
