A challenge market is how Kassandra settles the question "is this AI claim actually
correct?" without a human court. It is a **MetaDAO binary decision market** opened against a
single `AiClaim`: the proposer's already-locked KASS is conditional collateral, a challenger
posts USDC, and a fail-vs-pass **TWAP** read after a window delivers the verdict. This page
is the conceptual overview — the deep, account-by-account mechanics live under
[Challenge markets reference](/challenge/overview).

## Dormant by default

Markets cost nothing on an uncontested claim. An honest, unchallenged claim has **no
`Market` account and no trade** — the challenge machinery is dormant unless someone actually
disagrees (`open_challenge.rs`). This is the optimistic principle applied one level deeper:
the expensive path only fires on a real dispute.

## Opening a challenge

A challenger who has reproduced a claim and found it wrong opens a market with
`open_challenge` (`Ix::OpenChallenge = 4`) during `Phase::Challenge`. The MetaDAO market
accounts (a binary question, KASS and USDC conditional vaults, and pass/fail AMMs) are
**composed off-chain by the challenger** in their own transactions; `open_challenge` verifies
and records them, then (`challenge-markets` reference):

- **Splits the proposer's bond** into pass-KASS and fail-KASS via the conditional vault,
  program-signed by the oracle PDA. The bond's conditional tokens stay **idle** (never
  LP'd), so there is no impermanent loss on the bond.
- **Escrows challenger USDC** sized to the bond's value:
  `required_usdc = bond * kass_price / KASS_PRICE_SCALE`, where `kass_price` is the
  governance-anchored futarchy spot TWAP (`KASS_PRICE_SCALE = 1e12`). A zero requirement
  fails with `ZeroStake`.
- Records a `Market` PDA at `[b"market", ai_claim]`, sets `ai_claim.challenged = 1`, and
  increments `oracle.open_challenge_count`.

<Info>
  Outcome index **0 = pass** (the claim is honest) and **1 = fail** (the claim is fraud).
  Traders who believe the claim is wrong buy the **fail** pool, pushing fail-KASS up; those
  who believe it is honest hold the pass side. See [Trading](/challenge/trading).
</Info>

## The verdict: a fail-vs-pass TWAP

After a market's TWAP window (`market.twap_end = open_time + oracle.twap_window`), anyone
settles it with `settle_challenge` (`Ix::SettleChallenge = 5`). Settlement reads the stored
slot-weighted TWAP of each pool (no crank at settle) and applies the exact trigger
(`settle_challenge.rs:346-358`, computed in `u128`):

```
disqualify  iff  pass_twap != 0  AND  fail_twap * DEN > pass_twap * (DEN + NUM)
```

where `NUM/DEN` is the oracle's snapshotted relative margin (`market_threshold_num/den`,
default `1/10` — fail must beat pass by **more than 10%**). The comparison is **strict**:
at exact equality the claim **survives**.

<Warning>
  A **zero pass TWAP always survives** (the `pass_twap == 0` guard). A market with no
  counter-trading on the pass side cannot flip the claim — uncontested is treated as honest.
  Without this guard a challenger could crank only the fail pool and cheaply make
  `fail_twap * DEN > 0` true.
</Warning>

## Pass/fail split of the collateral

On settle, both paths resolve the MetaDAO question and physically redeem the bond KASS back
into the oracle's `stake_vault` (winning side 1:1, losing side to 0). The directional fees
differ:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Disqualify (fail wins — challenge succeeded)" icon="gavel">
    The proposer is disqualified and slashed: `bond − kass_fee` goes to `bond_pool`, a
    `kass_fee = bond * challenge_success_kass_fee` (default 1/100) goes to the challenger,
    the full USDC escrow returns to the challenger, and `surviving_count -= 1`.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Survive (pass wins — challenge failed)" icon="shield">
    The claim stands and the proposer keeps their bond. A `usdc_fee = challenger_usdc *
    challenge_fail_usdc_fee` (default 1/100) goes to the proposer as compensation; the USDC
    remainder returns to the challenger.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Single round, parallel, incremental

The challenge phase is a **single round** with **no cascade**. Every AI claim is
challengeable **in parallel** within the one window; each opened market trades for the
per-oracle TWAP duration. As each market settles it incrementally updates oracle state
(decrementing `open_challenge_count` and, on a disqualify, `surviving_count`) but does
**not** advance the phase — settling stays in `Challenge`. The last market's window is the
structural upper bound, so termination is guaranteed with no max-rounds cap (design §6,
invariant 2).

Only once `open_challenge_count == 0` can `finalize_oracle` (`Ix=6`) run the final plurality
recompute over surviving proposers and drive the oracle to `Resolved` or `InvalidDeadend`.
An attempt while challenges are outstanding fails with `ChallengesOutstanding`.

<Note>
  Challenge markets are built on **MetaDAO v0.4** conditional-vault and AMM programs, driven
  by CPI (the v0.4 AMM has the slot-weighted delayed-TWAP oracle Kassandra needs). The full
  compose sequence, TWAP mechanics, cranking, and settle account sets are documented in the
  challenge-markets reference.
</Note>
