The **proposal window** is where an oracle's answer is first put forward. It is the entire
happy path: if everyone who proposes agrees on one value, the oracle resolves here and now,
with no facts, no AI, and no markets. Only a genuine conflict escalates into a dispute.

## Registering a proposal

After the oracle's `deadline`, a participant registers a proposal with `propose`
(`Ix::Propose = 11`). The payload is exactly 9 bytes: `option: u8` plus `bond: u64 LE`
(`propose.rs`). The processor:

- escrows the KASS `bond` into the oracle's `stake_vault`, and
- creates one `Proposer` PDA per `(oracle, authority)` at seeds
  `[b"proposer", oracle, authority]`, recording `original_option = option` and
  `claim_option = CLAIM_OPTION_NONE` (0xFF, "no AI claim yet").

<ParamField path="option" type="u8">
  The categorical value being proposed, an index in `0..options_count`. Out of range fails
  with `InvalidOptionsCount`. **No proofs or evidence are attached** — the bond is the only
  thing backing the claim.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="bond" type="u64">
  The KASS bond (raw base units) escrowed into the stake vault. Must be positive
  (`ZeroStake`). It is what the proposer risks: it becomes conditional collateral in a
  challenge market and can be slashed.
</ParamField>

The key gates (`propose.rs`):

- **Phase must be `Proposal`** and `now >= deadline` (else `DeadlineNotReached`).
- **The window must be open.** Once it has closed with existing proposers, further
  proposals fail with `ProposalWindowClosed`.
- **One authority, one proposal.** A second proposal from the same authority fails with
  `DuplicateProposer` (the PDA already exists).
- **A hard cap of `MAX_PROPOSERS = 60`.** Exceeding it fails with `TooManyProposers`. The
  cap keeps the full proposer set finalizable within a single transaction's account budget.

<Note>
  **One proposer ↔ one value.** A proposer stakes a single option; they cannot hedge across
  options with one identity. Multiple opinions require multiple funded authorities — each
  putting real bond at risk.
</Note>

## The empty-window rule

If the proposal window would close with **zero proposals**, the oracle does not fail. The
first proposal instead *seeds* the value and (re)opens a normal bounded window — the
empty-window branch of `propose` extends `phase_ends_at` (`propose.rs`). There is no
"Unresolved from emptiness" terminal state; the oracle simply waits for its first real
proposer.

## Closing the window: agree or conflict

After the window elapses, anyone calls `finalize_proposals` (`Ix::FinalizeProposals = 12`),
passing the **full** proposer set (exactly `proposer_count` accounts). It is a one-shot
close with two outcomes (`finalize_proposals.rs`):

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="All agree → Resolved" icon="circle-check">
    Every proposer registered the same option. The oracle jumps straight to `Resolved` and
    stamps `resolved_option`. Bonds are returned and — on the happy path — no slashing
    occurs. No AI, no markets. This is the case Kassandra is optimized for.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Conflict → dispute" icon="scale-balanced">
    Two or more distinct options were proposed. The oracle opens a dispute: it fixes
    `dispute_bond_total = total_oracle_stake` (the sum of proposer bonds), advances to
    `FactProposal`, and opens a fresh window. Proposers are now **locked in**.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

If `proposer_count == 0` at finalize, the call fails with `NoProposals` — the empty-window
rule means a real oracle should always have at least one proposer before it can close.

<Warning>
  **Lock-in is irreversible.** The moment a conflict opens the dispute, proposer bonds are
  committed — they cannot be withdrawn (design invariant 4). A proposer who then abandons
  the dispute (never submits an AI claim) is **fully slashed**; abandoning mid-dispute is
  treated as likely responsible for the costly disagreement in the first place.
</Warning>

## Plurality is over proposers, not stake

`dispute_bond_total` — the sum of bonds — is used as a stable **denominator** for the
[fact quorum](/concepts/facts), not as voting weight. The final answer is decided by
**plurality over surviving proposers**: each surviving proposer contributes exactly one vote
for their (possibly updated) value, and the option with the strictly-highest count wins
(`plurality.rs`, evaluated at `finalize_oracle`).

<Info>
  Plurality is **one proposer, one vote** — deliberately *not* stake-weighted. A wealthy
  proposer risks more KASS, but does not get a louder vote. A tie between options has no
  winner and sends the oracle to an [Invalid dead-end](/concepts/dead-end-settlement).
</Info>
