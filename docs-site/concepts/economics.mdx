Kassandra's honesty guarantee is economic. Every claim is backed by KASS that can be
slashed; slashed KASS is redistributed to honest actors; new KASS is minted to honest
participants on a decaying curve; and a burned, demand-responsive creation fee is the
deflationary counterweight. This page ties those pieces to the real constants and formulas
in the code.

## Bonds and stakes

Everything staking uses **KASS**, escrowed into the oracle's `stake_vault` PDA:

- A **proposer bond** (`Proposer.bond`) backs a proposed value. It becomes conditional
  collateral in a challenge market and is what a proposer risks.
- A **fact-submission stake** (`Fact.stake`) backs a submitted fact.
- A **fact-vote stake** (`FactVote.stake`) is the weight a voter puts behind an approve or
  duplicate vote.

The oracle tracks a conservation accumulator, `total_oracle_stake`, which equals the vault
balance until a challenge splits a bond into a conditional vault. Design invariant 3 is
**conservation of KASS**: for any oracle,
`Σ stakes_in = Σ returned + Σ to_bond_pool + Σ burned + Σ emitted` — no KASS is created or
destroyed outside emissions (mint) and the fee/dead-end burns.

## Slashing and the bond pool

Slashed KASS flows into the `bond_pool` counter — an adversarial redistribution that makes
dishonesty net-negative. The slash paths:

| Path | Amount | Site |
|---|---|---|
| Rejected-fact submitter | full `fact.stake` (plus a slash on rejected-fact approve-voters) | `finalize_facts` |
| AI-claim no-show | full `bond` + disqualified | `finalize_ai_claims` |
| AI-claim flip | `bond * flip_slash_num / flip_slash_den` (default 1/2) | `finalize_ai_claims` |
| Challenge disqualify | `bond − kass_fee` | `settle_challenge` |

Each `Proposer` records its own `slashed_amount`, with the invariant that a proposer's
contribution to `bond_pool` equals its `slashed_amount` on every slash path. Duplicate-
dominant facts are **not** slashed — no penalty for honest redundancy.

## Rewards: buckets and pro-rata claims

On `Resolved` (only), the oracle stamps a distributable `reward_pool = bond_pool +
reward_emission` and splits it into two cohort buckets by config weights (`reward.rs`):

```
proposer_bucket = reward_pool * PW / (PW + FW)
fact_bucket     = reward_pool * FW / (PW + FW)
```

with default weights `PW = REWARD_PROPOSER_WEIGHT = 2` and `FW = REWARD_FACT_WEIGHT = 1`
(all division in `u128`, floored; floor dust stays in the vault). Each bucket is distributed
**pro-rata** within its cohort against a total stamped at resolution:

- `proposer_reward(bond) = bond * proposer_bucket / total_correct_proposer_stake` — for
  surviving proposers whose `claim_option == resolved_option`.
- `fact_reward(stake) = stake * fact_bucket / total_approved_fact_stake` — for agreed-fact
  submitters and approve-voters.

An **empty cohort's bucket rolls into** the other (there is always ≥1 winner on `Resolved`).
Claims are **per-participant pulls** after the oracle is terminal: `claim_proposer`,
`claim_fact`, and `claim_fact_vote` each compute an entitlement, pay KASS from the vault, and
close the account. No global iteration, no one-transaction cap.

<Info>
  On a dead-end there is **no reward** — `reward_pool == 0`, so every reward term is zero.
  Stakers get only their non-slashed principal back; slashed amounts and the emission are
  burned. See [Dead-end settlement](/concepts/dead-end-settlement).
</Info>

## Emissions: work-to-earn on a decaying curve

KASS is fair-launched: **no presale**, all supply enters via participation emissions. The
emission for an oracle is computed at **creation**, from the remaining reservoir
(`create_oracle.rs`):

```
reward_emission = (total_supply_cap − supply) * emission_num / emission_den   (u128 floor)
```

It is minted immediately into the oracle's `stake_vault` (signed by the `[b"mint_authority"]`
PDA), then folded into `reward_pool` on `Resolved` or **burned back** on `InvalidDeadend`.
Because the emission is a fixed fraction of the *remaining* reservoir, it is a **decaying
curve** — early honest participation is rewarded more and supply asymptotes toward
`total_supply_cap`. Recommended defaults are `EMISSION_NUM = 1`, `EMISSION_DEN = 1_000_000`
(1/1e6 of the reservoir per creation) and `TOTAL_SUPPLY_CAP = 1e18` (1e9 KASS at 9 decimals).

<Warning>
  Emissions are **disabled at genesis.** `init_protocol` sets `emission_num = 0`,
  `emission_den = 1`, and `total_supply_cap = 0` — it does **not** default to the
  `EMISSION_*`/`TOTAL_SUPPLY_CAP` consts. Emission is turned on later by governance via
  `set_config` (`init_protocol.rs`; program-instructions reference).
</Warning>

## The dynamic creation fee (KASS, burned)

Creating an oracle burns a **dynamic KASS fee** proportional to an EMA of recent creation
activity. The EMA (`Protocol.fee_ema`) is a fixed-point accumulator scaled by
`FEE_EMA_SCALE = 1e9` (so `fee_ema == 1e9` means 1.0 creation unit). Per creation the flow
is: **decay → charge → bump → stamp** (`fee.rs`, `create_oracle.rs`):

<Steps>
  <Step title="Decay by elapsed time">
    `decay_fee_ema(fee_ema, last_creation_unix, now)` halves the EMA once per
    `FEE_EMA_HALFLIFE_SECS = 86_400` (one day) with linear interpolation inside a half-life.
    It is monotonic non-increasing and never exceeds the stored value.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Charge the fee">
    `fee_for_ema(decayed) = FEE_PER_EMA_UNIT * decayed / FEE_EMA_SCALE` KASS
    (`FEE_PER_EMA_UNIT = 1e9`, i.e. 1 KASS per 1.0 EMA unit at 9 decimals). This KASS is
    **burned** — paid to no one.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Bump and stamp">
    `fee_ema = bumped_fee_ema(decayed) = decayed + FEE_EMA_INCREMENT` (one creation unit,
    `1e9`), and `last_creation_unix = now`.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The consequences: the fee is **0 at genesis** (`fee_ema` starts at 0, so early oracles are
free — you bootstrap in and earn KASS via emissions without acquiring it first); it **rises
with demand** as creations stack up; and it **relaxes as demand falls** through the daily
half-life decay. The burn balances emissions with no USDC floor. Fee monotonicity — the fee
moves only as a function of the creation-rate EMA and is never negative — is design
invariant 5.

## Governance

KASS governs protocol config. The behavioral and monetary params live on the `Protocol`
singleton and are updated wholesale by the DAO via `set_config` (`Ix=14`, bounds-checked).
They do **not** touch oracles already in flight, which read from their creation-time snapshot.
KASS governance (ideally via MetaDAO futarchy) is also the only authority that can resolve an
[Invalid dead-end](/concepts/dead-end-settlement) oracle.
