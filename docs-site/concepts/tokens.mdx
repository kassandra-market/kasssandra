Kassandra uses two tokens. **KASS** is the native protocol token — the medium for every
stake, bond, slash, reward, and the burned creation fee. **USDC** is the external collateral
a challenger posts to open a decision market. This page covers each token's role, what drives
demand for KASS, and how mint and burn stay in balance.

## KASS — the protocol token

KASS is an SPL token, pinned as the canonical `kass_mint` on the `Protocol` singleton and
snapshotted onto every `Oracle`. It has **no presale**; the entire supply enters through
participation **emissions** (a fair launch; design §8). Every KASS-denominated flow in the
protocol touches the oracle's `stake_vault`.

### What KASS is used for

  <Card title="Proposing" icon="hand">
    A proposer escrows a KASS `bond` to register a value. The bond is credible because it
    can be slashed — and it becomes conditional collateral if the claim is challenged.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Facts and votes" icon="list-check">
    Fact submitters escrow a KASS `stake`; fact voters escrow a KASS stake as their vote
    weight. Approving good evidence earns rewards; backing rejected facts is slashed.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Challenge collateral" icon="scale-balanced">
    A challenged proposer's already-locked KASS bond is split into pass/fail conditional

  </Card>
  <Card title="Governance" icon="landmark">
    KASS governs protocol config (thresholds, fee and emission params) and is the only
    authority that can resolve an [Invalid dead-end](/concepts/dead-end-settlement) —
    ideally via MetaDAO futarchy.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### Demand drivers

You must hold and stake KASS to participate at all: to propose a value, to stake on facts,
and — as a proposer — it *is* your conditional market collateral (design §8). Demand scales
with oracle activity: more disputes mean more bonds and stakes locked, and a higher dynamic
creation fee burning more KASS per oracle.

### Supply: fair-launch via emissions

The supply enters through **work-to-earn** emissions minted to honest participants
(correct proposers, approved-fact stakers, and successful challengers). Emission for an
oracle is a fixed fraction of the *remaining* reservoir, computed at creation:
`reward_emission = (total_supply_cap − supply) * emission_num / emission_den`
(`create_oracle.rs`). Because it draws down a shrinking reservoir, it is a **decaying curve**
— early honest participation is rewarded more, and supply asymptotes toward
`total_supply_cap` (recommended `1e18` = 1e9 KASS at 9 decimals). Emissions are **disabled at
genesis** and turned on later by governance. See [Economics](/concepts/economics).

## USDC — challenge-market collateral

USDC is the external stable collateral used only in the challenge phase. A challenger opens a
decision market by posting USDC, sized to the proposer's bond value at the current KASS price:
`required_usdc = bond * kass_price / KASS_PRICE_SCALE`, where `kass_price` is the
governance-anchored futarchy spot TWAP (`KASS_PRICE_SCALE = 1e12`; `open_challenge.rs`). The
USDC is escrowed in a market-owned PDA and split into pass/fail conditional USDC to seed the
AMM pools. At settlement it is routed directionally:

- **Challenge succeeds (disqualify):** the full USDC escrow returns to the challenger.
- **Challenge fails (survive):** a small `usdc_fee` (default 1/100) goes to the proposer as
  compensation; the remainder returns to the challenger.

The USDC mint is pinned per oracle as `usdc_mint` (must match the Protocol's canonical USDC).
See [Challenge economics](/challenge/economics) for the full split.

## The burn-vs-mint balance

KASS supply is shaped by two opposing forces, both demand-responsive:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Mint — emissions" icon="arrow-up">
    New KASS is minted to honest participants on the decaying reservoir curve, rewarding the
    work that makes the oracle trustworthy.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Burn — the creation fee (and dead-end burns)" icon="fire">
    Every oracle creation burns a dynamic KASS fee proportional to a demand EMA (0 at
    genesis, rising with activity). On a dead-end, slashed amounts and the emission are burned
    too. The burn is the deflationary counterweight to emissions, with **no USDC floor**.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

Design invariant 3 keeps the whole system closed: for any oracle,
`Σ stakes_in = Σ returned + Σ to_bond_pool + Σ burned + Σ emitted`. No KASS is created or
destroyed outside emissions (mint) and the fee/dead-end burns.
