A challenge is a wager with real, comparable skin-in-the-game on both sides: the
proposer's locked **KASS bond** is the conditional collateral, and the challenger must
**escrow USDC** worth roughly the same amount. The market decides who is right, and
settlement moves value between them plus small directional fees. This page covers where
every unit goes and why the incentives make honest, uncontested claims the default.

For the broader protocol economics — bonds, emissions, and the dynamic fee — see
[Concepts: economics](/concepts/economics).

## The two stakes

    pass-KASS + fail-KASS (never LP'd → no impermanent loss). Slashed if the market
    disqualifies the proposer.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Challenger's escrow (USDC)" icon="vault">
    Posted at `open_challenge` into a market-owned SPL escrow at PDA
    `[b"challenge_usdc", market]` (token authority = the oracle PDA). Sized to the
    bond's KASS value so both sides risk comparable amounts.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Sizing the escrow via `kass_price`

`open_challenge` computes the required escrow from the bond and the governance-anchored
KASS spot price (`open_challenge.rs:354-433`):

```text
required_usdc = bond × twap / KASS_PRICE_SCALE
twap          = kass_price(protocol, kass_dao)     // raw USDC per raw KASS × 1e12
KASS_PRICE_SCALE = 1_000_000_000_000               // 1e12
```

`kass_price` is the v0.6 futarchy embedded spot TWAP, anchored to `Protocol.kass_dao`.
Its `1e12` scale already folds in the KASS 9 dp ↔ USDC 6 dp decimal difference, so **no
extra `10^Δdec` factor** is needed. Dimensionally `[KASS_raw] × [USDC_raw / KASS_raw] =
[USDC_raw]`.

<Info>
  **Worked example** (from the C1 delta log). KASS at $0.50 → `twap = 500_000_000`. A
  1 KASS bond (`1e9` base units) escrows `1e9 × 5e8 / 1e12 = 500_000` USDC base units =
  $0.50. Both sides then have ≈$0.50 at risk.
</Info>

The escrow is funded by a **challenger-signed** SPL `Transfer`; an under-funded source
makes the transfer fail and the whole instruction reverts. `required_usdc == 0` (a
sub-micro bond that truncates to nothing) is rejected with `ZeroStake` — otherwise there
would be no fee source at settle.

## Where the value goes at settle

Both outcomes first `redeem_tokens` the idle bond 1:1 back into `oracle.stake_vault`
(see [Settlement](/challenge/settle)). Then:

### Disqualify — challenge SUCCEEDED (fail-win)

| Flow | Amount | Destination |
| --- | --- | --- |
| KASS fee (challenger reward) | `bond × challenge_success_kass_fee_num/den` (default `1/100`) | challenger KASS (from `stake_vault`) |
| Slashed bond | `bond − kass_fee` | proposer's `bond_pool` contribution (`== slashed_amount`) |
| USDC escrow | full `challenger_usdc` | returned to challenger |

`surviving_count -= 1`; `proposer.disqualified = slashed = 1`. The challenger risked USDC
and gets it **all** back plus a KASS reward carved out of the slashed bond; the rest of
the bond is slashed into the pool.

### Survive — challenge FAILED (pass-win)

| Flow | Amount | Destination |
| --- | --- | --- |
| USDC fee (proposer reward) | `challenger_usdc × challenge_fail_usdc_fee_num/den` (default `1/100`) | proposer USDC |
| USDC remainder | `challenger_usdc − usdc_fee` | returned to challenger |
| Bond | full `bond` | stays the proposer's (in `stake_vault`) |

The proposer keeps their whole bond and pockets a small USDC fee from the challenger's
escrow as compensation for the nuisance; the challenger gets their escrow back minus that
fee.

<Note>
  The directional-fee rates are **governable** per-oracle snapshots
  (`challenge_fail_usdc_fee_num/den`, `challenge_success_kass_fee_num/den`), defaulting to
  `1/100 = 1%`. They are snapshotted from `Protocol` at oracle creation and updated via
  `set_config` under bounds (`den > 0`, `num ≤ den`).
</Note>

## Conservation

The rework holds two exact conservation identities, verified end-to-end against an
independent reference model (`docs/plans/2026-06-29-kassandra-challenge-rework.md`, C3):

```text
USDC:  proposer_usdc + challenger_usdc_dest == escrow                     // both outcomes
KASS (survive):     stake_vault + kass_vault_underlying == total_oracle_stake
KASS (disqualify):  stake_vault + kass_vault_underlying + challenger_kass == total_oracle_stake
```

On disqualify the `kass_fee` physically leaves `stake_vault` to the challenger, so
`total_oracle_stake` becomes an **idealized accumulator, not physical KASS** — the
carve-out is the difference. The per-proposer identity `slashed_amount == bond_pool
contribution == bond − kass_fee` is the authoritative slash figure; settlement always
sources payouts from real `stake_vault` / `bond_pool` balances and this ledger, never
from `total_oracle_stake` or live token-account balances.

<Warning>
  **The bond is never AMM liquidity.** It is split into idle conditional tokens and
  redeemed 1:1 — so slashing is clean and KASS is conserved. Market liquidity and its
  impermanent-loss risk belong entirely to the challenger and traders, never the bond.
</Warning>

## The game theory

- **Uncontested = honest by assumption.** With no challenge, there is no `Market` and no
  cost — the common case is free. The machinery only fires when someone stakes real
  money on "this is fraud."
- **Symmetric skin-in-the-game.** The escrow is sized to the bond via `kass_price`, so a
  frivolous challenge risks USDC comparable to the bond it attacks.
- **The cost of griefing.** To disqualify an honest proposer, a challenger must drive
  `fail_twap * DEN > pass_twap * (DEN + NUM)` — a *sustained* fail price cranked across
  the window (see [Cranking](/challenge/cranking)) — against anyone who counter-trades
  pass. The `pass_twap == 0` guard means an honest claim that nobody bothers to defend
  still survives, so a griefer cannot win by cranking fail alone. If the challenge fails,
  the challenger forfeits the `usdc_fee` to the proposer and recovers only the remainder.
- **The reward for catching fraud.** A successful challenge returns the challenger's full
  escrow **and** pays a KASS fee carved from the slashed bond — a positive expected value
  only when the claim really is fraudulent and the market agrees.

## Deferred (rent)

`settle_challenge` sets `market.settled = 1` but does **not** close the `Market` PDA or
the `challenger_usdc_vault` escrow — the challenger's rent stays locked until a
settlement-era close instruction (`close_market`, Ix=21), mirroring the Proposer/Fact
non-closure convention.
